Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about Medicare and insurance in Georgia

What areas do you serve in Georgia?

I serve clients throughout Metro Atlanta and North Georgia, including Marietta, Roswell, Kennesaw, Alpharetta, Smyrna, Acworth, Woodstock, and surrounding areas in Cobb County, Fulton County, and Cherokee County.

What types of Medicare plans do you offer?

I help clients compare and enroll in Medicare Advantage plans, Medicare Supplement (Medigap) plans, Medicare Part D prescription drug plans, and provide guidance on Original Medicare options.

Do you charge fees for your services?

No, my consultation services are completely free. As an independent insurance agent, I'm compensated by insurance carriers when you enroll in a plan, so there's no cost to you for my expertise and guidance.

When can I enroll in Medicare?

You can enroll during your Initial Enrollment Period (7 months around your 65th birthday), Annual Open Enrollment (October 15 - December 7), or if you qualify for a Special Enrollment Period due to life changes like moving or losing other coverage.

What's the difference between Medicare Advantage and Medicare Supplement?

Medicare Advantage (Part C) replaces Original Medicare and often includes extras like dental and vision. Medicare Supplement (Medigap) works alongside Original Medicare to help cover out-of-pocket costs like deductibles and copayments.

Do you help with health insurance for people under 65?

Yes! I help individuals and families find health insurance through the Health Insurance Marketplace, including ACA-compliant plans with essential health benefits. I also offer guidance on short-term medical plans and supplemental coverage options.
